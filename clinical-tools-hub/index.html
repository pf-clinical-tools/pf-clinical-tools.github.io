Clinical Decision Support Tools

These tools are designed to support clinical decision-making and promote consistent, safe practice. They do not replace clinical judgement, local policy, or specialist advice. Use within appropriate clinical governance frameworks and escalate where uncertainty or patient-specific factors exist.

1. Anticoagulation Risk Assessment Tool in NVAF

Assists clinicians in assessing stroke risk and anticoagulation considerations in patients with non-valvular atrial fibrillation, using structured, guideline-aligned prompts. Designed to support balanced, conservative decision-making where bleeding risk or uncertainty exists.

Open Anticoagulation Risk Assessment Tool (NVAF)

2. Bedside Diastolic Dysfunction Assessment Tool

Provides a structured bedside approach to grading diastolic dysfunction using standard echocardiographic parameters, with emphasis on confidence estimation and clinical context. Intended for clinicians assessing unexplained dyspnoea or suspected heart failure.

Open Diastolic Dysfunction Assessment Tool

3. Peri-operative Catheterisation and TWOC Decision Aid in Femoral and Pelvic Fracture Patients

Supports nursing and medical staff in making safe, auditable decisions regarding urinary catheterisation and trial without catheter (TWOC) in patients with femoral or pelvic fractures. Focuses on minimising unnecessary catheter use while reducing catheter-associated harm.

Open Catheterisation & TWOC Decision Aid

4. Peri-operative PE / DVT Management Decision Support Tool (Femoral Fractures)

Provides a structured framework to help clinicians balance thrombotic and bleeding risks in patients with acute pulmonary embolism or deep vein thrombosis who require urgent femoral fracture surgery. Designed to support multidisciplinary peri-operative decision-making.

Open Peri-operative PE / DVT Decision Support Tool
